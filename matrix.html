Matrix

Definition: A Matrix is a table of math objects.

For example a matrix $\mathbf{A} \in \mathbb K^{2\times 3}$ could be $\mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \end{bmatrix}$.

$\mathbf{A}=(a_{ij}) \in \mathbb K^{m\times n}$ has $m$ rows (index $i$) and $n$ columns (index $j$)

| Dimension | |-----------| | $\dim \mathbb K = n = \text{rang}\mathbf{A} + \dim\,\text{ker}\mathbf{A}$ | | $\text{rang}\mathbf{A} = \text{rang}\mathbf{A}^{\top}$ |

Calculations

$(\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B})^\top = \mathbf{A}^\top + \mathbf{B}^\top$ | $(\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B})^\top = \mathbf{B}^\top \cdot \mathbf{A}^\top$ |
${(\mathbf{A}^\top)}^{-1} = {(\mathbf{A}^{-1})}^\top$ | $(\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B})^{-1} = \mathbf{B}^{-1}\mathbf{A}^{-1}$ |

Quadratic Matrices

Properties
regular $\Leftrightarrow \det (\mathbf{A}) \ne 0 \Leftrightarrow \text{rang}\mathbf{A} = n$
singular $\Leftrightarrow \det (\mathbf{A}) = 0 \Leftrightarrow \text{rang}\mathbf{A} \ne n$
orthogonal $\Leftrightarrow \mathbf{A}^\top=\mathbf{A}^{-1} \Rightarrow\det(\mathbf{A}) = \pm 1$
symmetric $\mathbf{A}=\mathbf{A}^\top$ schiefsymmetrisch: $\mathbf{A}=-\mathbf{A}^\top$

Rotation Matrix of $\R^3$

for rotations in a right-handed coordinate system $XYZ$.

Rotation on x-axis:

$$\mathbf{R}_x(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos \alpha & -\sin \alpha \\ 0 & \sin \alpha & \cos \alpha \end{bmatrix}$$

$$\mathbf{R}_y(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \alpha & 0 & \sin \alpha \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin \alpha & 0 & \cos \alpha \end{bmatrix}$$

$$\mathbf{R}_{z}(\alpha )=\begin{pmatrix}\cos \alpha &-\sin \alpha &0\\\sin \alpha &\cos \alpha &0\\0&0&1\end{bmatrix}$$

Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Eigenvalues: $\det(\mathbf{A} - \lambda \mathbf{1}) = 0$
Eigenvectors: $\,\text{ker}(\mathbf{A} - \lambda_i \mathbf{1}) = \vec v_i$

$\mathbf{A} \vec v = \lambda \vec v$ $\det \mathbf{A} = \prod \lambda_i$  $\,\text{Sp}\mathbf{A} = \sum a_{ii} = \sum \lambda_i$

Special 2×2 Matrices

$\det(\mathbf{A}) = ad-bc$   $\,\text{Sp}(\mathbf{A}) = a+d$
$\begin{bmatrix} a & b \ c & d \end{bmatrix}^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det \mathbf{A}} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b\ -c& a \end{bmatrix}$

$\lambda_{1/2} = \frac{\,\text{Sp}\mathbf{A}}{2} \pm \sqrt{ \left( \frac{\,\text{Sp}\mathbf{A}}{2} \right)^2 - \det \mathbf{A} }$
